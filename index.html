A bit about me!

"Llamas can drive... they just don't know it yet..."
-Llama Queen

I first encountered Llamas when I was around four years old. They were so cute and cuddly, they were protectors of the herd and they regarded me as such... until that one fateful day. I was in my car when the asteroid came down, I saw the asteroid streak across the sky, in a great spectacle of orange of purple. I remember getting out of the car and looking in the direction it was heading towards, when a bright purple light enveloped the sky, the signs that the asteroid had impacted. In the following days the speculation was that this asteroid was harmless, and that the following cloudy days that resounded world-wide were merely because of the disturbance in the atmosphere. While most bought this explanation, I decided to put my web development career on break in order to do some research, and what I found astonished me. After receiving very few answers about the impact of this asteroid on this plante, I went to my parents' house to take a break and get away from my research. I went out to the pastures with the sheep and goats to say hello to my llama friends/protectors, but they regarded me with a gaze similar to the one that they used on predators that threatened their flock. But there was one small difference in their gaze, it was predatory, they saw me as a meal, and seemed to be staring directly into my soul.
I began to go around to the other pastures of our neighbors and found that the look was the exact same, and there began my research...

These are the dangers we face: Llamas.

These are the Llama's you need to worry about. Each one has a description and a challenge rating for dealing with them.

The Yelling Llama

Challenge Rating: 5/5

Identified by its incessant yelling, this one is calmed by a mid-2nd century music box, and thus has the most difficult challenge rating.

Fuzzy Llama

Challenge Rating: 3/5

Identified by its fuzzy head, this one usually enjoys scritches on the top of the head.

The Chewing Llama

Challenge Rating: 2/5

Identified by constantly having one piece of straw in its mouth but seeming to never complete eating it. This exact piece of straw must be dipped into gold and then offered back on a silver platter

The Llama of the Ruins

Challenge Rating: 4/5

These Llamas like to remain in close proximity to ruins. These just need to be given a piece of the ruins as an offering, but beware, they do try to guard the ruins

The Spotted Llama

Challenge Rating: 1/5

With a challenge rating of 1, these are the easiest to appease. All one needs to do is scritch each spot in descending order from the top of the head to the hoofs (if done out of order, just startr over).

The Llama Pack

Challenge Rating: 2/5

Characerized by always being near other Llamas, the pack is difficult to deal with. This pack can only be appeased by dressing like one and spending an entire year in and amongst the pack

The Old School Llama

Challenge Rating: 5/5

When trying to photograph this type of llama, it will always make the image black and white, and there is nothing you can do about this until it is tamed. In order to appease this type, show it a color wheel in order to broaden its horizons

The Lying Llama

Challenge Rating: 3/5

This type of Llama will lie down all the time. In order to appease it, you must run circles around it for 5 minutes, 5 times a day, for 3 months, while yelling complete lies to it. If you repeat a lie, you must start the 3 months over again.
